Scoring methodology

Each player receives a base dynasty value from our embedded database (calibrated to 2024-25 KTC rankings), then a performance score computed from position-specific criteria weighted below. Dynasty value = ((KTC × age decay × startProb) + criteria adjustment) × superflex premium. Weights are derived from three research-grounded factors: year-over-year stat stability (35%), half-PPR fantasy points correlation (45%), and independent signal strength (20%). TD rate and red zone stats receive low weight — they are the most volatile, highest-regression metrics in football analytics. EPA-based metrics and separation/YPRR receive highest weight as they are the stickiest predictors. RBs use a position-specific age decay curve with a steep cliff: full value at 22-23, dropping to 40% at 28 and near-zero at 32+. WRs and QBs use a shallower decay that doesn't begin until their statistical peak age. In superflex, QBs receive an 18% positional premium and RBs receive a 5% penalty — reflecting scarcity dynamics in 2-QB leagues.
